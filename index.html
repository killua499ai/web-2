The Philippines is uniquely vulnerable to climate change. Since the 1800s, human activities like burning fossil fuels have become the main driver of global warming.

Climate Change Info

Understanding Climate Change

Climate change refers to long‑term shifts in temperature, precipitation, and weather patterns. While natural processes have always influenced the climate, the rapid warming we see today is driven almost entirely by human activities—mainly from burning fossil fuels, deforestation, industrial pollution, and unsustainable land use. The Philippines, located in the Pacific Ring of Fire and typhoon belt, faces intensified storms, rising sea levels, hotter temperatures, and threats to food security.

Scientific Basis

The Industrial Catalyst

"Mining is responsible for 7% of subtropical deforestation." — Global Forest Atlas

Fossil Fuels

The combustion process to provide energy causes a drastic release of carbon dioxide.

Mining

Methane trapped in coal seams is released during extraction and in underground mines.

CFC Gases

Refrigerants released from old leaking refrigerators and air conditioners.

Volcanoes

A single eruption releases massive amounts of CO2 and ash into the atmosphere.

Core Mitigation Strategies

Energy Decarbonization

Transitioning from fossil fuels to renewable sources like solar, wind, and geothermal.

  • Solar Farm Integration
  • Wind Energy Expansion

Nature-Based Solutions

Combatting deforestation through reforestation and ecosystem restoration.

  • Mangrove Restoration
  • Protected Forest Zones

Methane Capture

Implementing technology in mining sites and landfills to capture methane gas.

  • Mining Vent Filtration
  • Landfill Gas-to-Energy
